Walking with children
Are your children not keen on walking? Turn your walking route into an adventure trail and allow your children to discover nature! Here a few helpful tips on how to accomplish this:
  • Plan your trips so they can be spontaneously shortened or changed.
  • Before you set out on long hikes, test the stamina and pace of your children on short walks.
  • Don't set your heights too high or try to achieve a specific goal at all costs.
  • Little things along the edge of the trail are probably more interesting to children than the mountain panorama.
  • Allow your children to discover, collect and observe things on their own.
  • Make sure you take enough breaks and that you have sufficient food and drink with you.
  • Children usually find it great fun to carry their own, lightweight, backpack.

The right gear:
  • Sturdy hiking boots with a good tread
  • Robust, functional clothing that can also get dirty
  • Don't forget to take some spare clothes
